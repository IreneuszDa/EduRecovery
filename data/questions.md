# Quick Screening Questions

## Fractions
**Question ID:** Q-FRAC-01
**Category:** Fractions
**Curriculum Reference:** MATH-5-FRAC
**Difficulty:** Easy
**Question:** Which of the following is equivalent to 3/4?
**Options:**
A) 6/8
B) 4/5
C) 9/16
D) 5/8
**Correct Answer:** A
**Short Explanation:** Multiplying both the numerator (3) and denominator (4) by 2 gives 6/8.
**Teacher Note:** If the student chooses C, they might be squaring the numbers instead of scaling them linearly. If they choose D, they might just be adding 2 to the numerator and 4 to the denominator incorrectly.

**Question ID:** Q-FRAC-02
**Category:** Fractions
**Curriculum Reference:** MATH-5-FRAC
**Difficulty:** Medium
**Question:** What is 1/2 + 1/3?
**Options:**
A) 2/5
B) 5/6
C) 1/6
D) 1/5
**Correct Answer:** B
**Short Explanation:** To add fractions, find a common denominator (6). 1/2 becomes 3/6 and 1/3 becomes 2/6. 3/6 + 2/6 = 5/6.
**Teacher Note:** Answering A indicates the common misconception of adding numerators and denominators straight across. This suggests a foundational gap in understanding common denominators.

## Percentages
**Question ID:** Q-PERC-01
**Category:** Percentages
**Curriculum Reference:** MATH-5-PERC
**Difficulty:** Medium
**Question:** What is 20% of 50?
**Options:**
A) 5
B) 10
C) 20
D) 25
**Correct Answer:** B
**Short Explanation:** 20% is equivalent to 1/5. 1/5 of 50 is 10. Or, 0.20 * 50 = 10.
**Teacher Note:** If they answer C, they may just be repeating the percentage number. If they answer A, they might be dividing by 10 instead of finding 20%.

**Question ID:** Q-PERC-02
**Category:** Percentages
**Curriculum Reference:** MATH-5-PERC
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Question:** A shirt originally costs 400 UAH. It is on sale for 25% off. What is the sale price?
**Options:**
A) 100 UAH
B) 300 UAH
C) 375 UAH
D) 500 UAH
**Correct Answer:** B
**Short Explanation:** 25% of 400 is 100. The sale price is the original price minus the discount: 400 - 100 = 300 UAH.
**Teacher Note:** Answering A means the student found the discount amount but forgot to subtract it from the original price. This is a common gap in applying percentages to real-world multi-step problems.

## Geometry
**Question ID:** Q-GEOM-01
**Category:** Geometry
**Curriculum Reference:** MATH-5-GEOM
**Difficulty:** Easy
**Question:** What is the area of a rectangle with a length of 8 cm and a width of 5 cm?
**Options:**
A) 13 cm²
B) 26 cm²
C) 40 cm²
D) 85 cm²
**Correct Answer:** C
**Short Explanation:** The area of a rectangle is length multiplied by width (8 * 5 = 40).
**Teacher Note:** Answering B (26) means the student calculated the perimeter instead of the area. This indicates a mix-up between the concepts of area and perimeter.

**Question ID:** Q-GEOM-02
**Category:** Geometry
**Curriculum Reference:** MATH-5-GEOM
**Difficulty:** Medium
**Question:** The sum of the angles in a triangle is always:
**Options:**
A) 90 degrees
B) 180 degrees
C) 270 degrees
D) 360 degrees
**Correct Answer:** B
**Short Explanation:** The interior angles of any triangle always add up to 180 degrees.
**Teacher Note:** Answering D (360) suggests the student is confusing the rule for triangles with the rule for quadrilaterals (like squares or rectangles).

## Equations
**Question ID:** Q-EQ-01
**Category:** Equations
**Curriculum Reference:** MATH-6-EQ
**Difficulty:** Medium
**Question:** Solve for x: 2x + 4 = 10
**Options:**
A) x = 3
B) x = 4
C) x = 6
D) x = 7
**Correct Answer:** A
**Short Explanation:** Subtract 4 from both sides to get 2x = 6. Divide by 2 to get x = 3.
**Teacher Note:** Answering C (6) indicates the student might have only subtracted 4 but forgot to divide by 2, or they added 4 to 10 instead. This shows a gap in isolating variables step-by-step.

**Question ID:** Q-EQ-02
**Category:** Equations
**Curriculum Reference:** MATH-6-EQ
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Question:** If y - 5 = 12, what is the value of y + 2?
**Options:**
A) 7
B) 14
C) 17
D) 19
**Correct Answer:** D
**Short Explanation:** First, solve for y: y = 12 + 5 = 17. Then substitute y back in: 17 + 2 = 19.
**Teacher Note:** Answering C means the student successfully solved for y (17) but forgot to complete the second step of the question. This indicates an issue with problem-reading rather than equation solving.

## Word Problems
**Question ID:** Q-WP-01
**Category:** Word Problems
**Curriculum Reference:** MATH-56-WP
**Difficulty:** Medium
**Question:** Maria has 24 apples. She gives 1/3 of them to her friend. How many apples does Maria have left?
**Options:**
A) 8
B) 12
C) 16
D) 21
**Correct Answer:** C
**Short Explanation:** 1/3 of 24 is 8. Maria gave away 8 apples. 24 - 8 = 16 apples left.
**Teacher Note:** Answering A (8) means the student calculated the amount given away but forgot to subtract it from the total. This is a common gap in reading comprehension for multi-step word problems.

**Question ID:** Q-WP-02
**Category:** Word Problems
**Curriculum Reference:** MATH-56-WP
**Difficulty:** Medium
**Question:** A train travels 120 kilometers in 2 hours. At this speed, how far will it travel in 5 hours?
**Options:**
A) 240 km
B) 300 km
C) 600 km
D) 120 km
**Correct Answer:** B
**Short Explanation:** The speed is 120 / 2 = 60 km per hour. In 5 hours, the train travels 60 * 5 = 300 km.
**Teacher Note:** Answering C means they likely multiplied 120 by 5 directly without finding the unit rate first. This indicates a gap in understanding rates and proportions.
