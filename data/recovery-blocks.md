# Recovery Plan Blocks

These remediation blocks provide the content for the 14-day plan based on the student's identified learning gaps.

## Category: Fractions
* **Curriculum Expectation Missed:** [MATH-5-FRAC] Учень/учениця розуміє сутність звичайного дробу; виконує додавання і віднімання дробів з однаковими знаменниками; розв'язує сюжетні задачі, що передбачають знаходження дробу від числа.
* **Learning Goal:** Understand fractions as parts of a whole, find equivalent fractions, and perform basic addition with common denominators.
* **Teacher Explanation:** "Fractions often break down when students memorize procedures (like 'adding straight across') without visualizing the math. We need to step back and use physical or visual models to rebuild the concept of a 'whole' and its parts."
* **Activities:**
  1. Paper folding: Create visual fractions using folded paper strips to show equivalence (e.g., 1/2 = 2/4).
  2. Number line placement: Draw a large number line from 0 to 1 and place various fractions on it.
  3. Recipe scaling: Halve or double a simple recipe.
* **Low-Tech Adaptation:** Use sticks, drawn chalk circles, or torn pieces of paper to represent fractions. No printed worksheets required.
* **Printable Exercises:**
  1. "Shade the shape to show the equivalent fraction."
  2. "Place these 5 fractions on the number line."
  3. "Add the shaded parts together and write the new fraction."
* **Mini-Check Question:** "Draw a picture that proves 1/3 is the same as 2/6."

## Category: Percentages
* **Curriculum Expectation Missed:** [MATH-5-PERC] Учень/учениця розуміє відсоток як соту частину величини; перетворює відсотки у дроби і навпаки; знаходить відсоток від числа у задачах з реального життя.
* **Learning Goal:** Connect percentages to fractions (out of 100) and solve simple real-world percentage problems (like discounts).
* **Teacher Explanation:** "Students often struggle with percentages because they don't see them as 'a special fraction out of 100'. Grounding percentages in real-world scenarios like shopping discounts makes the abstract concept concrete."
* **Activities:**
  1. 10x10 Grid shading: Shade squares on a 100-grid to show 10%, 25%, 50%.
  2. The 10% trick: Practice finding 10% of any number by moving the decimal, then use it to find 20% or 30%.
  3. Store flyer math: Use a local grocery flyer to calculate 20% off sales.
* **Low-Tech Adaptation:** Draw a "battery charge" bar on the board. Ask students to estimate 25%, 50%, and 75% charge levels.
* **Printable Exercises:**
  1. "Match the percentage to the fraction (e.g., 25% -> 1/4)."
  2. "Calculate 10% and 20% of the following numbers."
  3. "Find the final price of an 80 UAH item with a 25% discount."
* **Mini-Check Question:** "If a phone is 50% charged and the battery holds 10 hours, how many hours are left?"

## Category: Geometry
* **Curriculum Expectation Missed:** [MATH-5-GEOM] Учень/учениця розрізняє периметр і площу; застосовує формули для обчислення площі прямокутника; знає, що сума кутів трикутника дорівнює 180°.
* **Learning Goal:** Distinguish between perimeter (border) and area (space inside), and understand the sum of angles in basic shapes.
* **Teacher Explanation:** "The most common geometry gap at this age is confusing area and perimeter. Rote memorization of formulas fails here. Students need to physically measure borders and count square units inside shapes."
* **Activities:**
  1. Room measurement: Pace out the perimeter of the classroom, then estimate the floor area in 'floor tiles'.
  2. String shapes: Use a fixed length of string to create rectangles of different areas.
  3. Triangle tear: Cut out a paper triangle, tear off the three corners, and line them up to prove they make a straight line (180 degrees).
* **Low-Tech Adaptation:** Count the perimeter and area of a desk using hands or notebooks as units of measurement.
* **Printable Exercises:**
  1. "Calculate the perimeter and area of these three rectangles."
  2. "Draw two different rectangles that both have an area of 12."
  3. "Find the missing third angle of these triangles."
* **Mini-Check Question:** "Explain the difference between perimeter and area using a fence and a garden."

## Category: Equations
* **Curriculum Expectation Missed:** [MATH-6-EQ] Учень/учениця розуміє рівняння як рівність двох виразів; застосовує основні властивості рівнянь для їх розв'язування.
* **Learning Goal:** Understand the equal sign as a balance point and isolate variables using inverse operations.
* **Teacher Explanation:** "Many students see the '=' sign as 'the answer comes next' rather than a balance scale. We must teach them that whatever you do to one side of the equation, you must do to the other to keep it balanced."
* **Activities:**
  1. Balance scale drawing: Represent equations like `x + 3 = 7` as weights on a drawn scale.
  2. The "Undo" game: Teacher says an operation ("I multiplied by 3"), student says the inverse ("I divide by 3 to undo it").
  3. Step-by-step solving: Write equations large on a board and use different colors to show the inverse operation applied to both sides.
* **Low-Tech Adaptation:** Use small stones and two paper plates to physically balance "equations" before writing numbers.
* **Printable Exercises:**
  1. "Fill in the missing step to solve the equation."
  2. "Solve these 5 one-step equations."
  3. "Solve these 3 two-step equations."
* **Mini-Check Question:** "What is the first step to solve 3x - 4 = 11?"

## Category: Word Problems
* **Curriculum Expectation Missed:** [MATH-56-WP] Учень/учениця аналізує умову задачі, виокремлює відомі та невідомі величини; встановлює залежності між ними; розв'язує задачу в кілька дій або за допомогою рівняння.
* **Learning Goal:** Break down multi-step text problems, identify the necessary operations, and check if the answer makes logical sense.
* **Teacher Explanation:** "Word problems test reading comprehension as much as math. Students often just grab the numbers and guess an operation. We need to teach them to visualize the story first before doing any math."
* **Activities:**
  1. Numberless word problems: Read a problem with the numbers blacked out. Discuss only *what is happening* and *what operation is needed*.
  2. Draw the story: Have students sketch a quick comic strip of the math problem before solving it.
  3. Write your own: Students write a word problem for a given equation (e.g., write a story for 15 - 8 = 7).
* **Low-Tech Adaptation:** Orally tell a story problem. Have students act it out or draw it on the chalkboard.
* **Printable Exercises:**
  1. "Underline the question and circle the important numbers."
  2. "Match the word problem to the correct equation."
  3. "Solve these two multi-step problems and explain your reasoning."
* **Mini-Check Question:** "Read this problem. Don't solve it, just tell me: will the answer be bigger or smaller than the starting number?"
