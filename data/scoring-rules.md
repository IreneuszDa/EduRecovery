# Scoring Rules

## Overview
The EduRecovery quick screening uses deterministic scoring to provide an immediate, practical recommendation for educators. It is a "quick screening" designed to find a starting point for recovery learning, not a clinical or high-stakes diagnostic assessment.

## Category Scoring
The screening consists of 10 questions, divided evenly into 5 categories (2 questions per category).

For each category, the score can be:
* **2 / 2 (100%):** Strong area.
* **1 / 2 (50%):** Partial understanding (potential gap).
* **0 / 2 (0%):** Likely learning gap.

## Weakest-Category Detection
1. **Calculate score** for all 5 categories.
2. **Sort categories** by score in ascending order (0/2, then 1/2, then 2/2).
3. **Select the top two** categories with the lowest scores to form the basis of the 14-day recovery plan.

## Tie-Breaking Rules
If three or more categories have the same lowest score, apply the following tie-breaking hierarchy (based on foundational skill dependencies):
1. **Fractions** (most foundational)
2. **Percentages** (builds on fractions)
3. **Equations** (core algebraic thinking)
4. **Geometry** (often stands slightly apart, but uses algebraic concepts)
5. **Word Problems** (application of other skills)

*Example:* If a student scores 0/2 in Percentages, Equations, and Word Problems, the system selects Percentages and Equations as the two primary gaps to address first.

## Confidence Labels
To avoid overclaiming assessment validity, results use confidence labels rather than definitive judgments:
* **0/2 in a category:** "Strong gap signal" – Highly likely this is a missing foundational block. Recommended as an immediate restart point.
* **1/2 in a category:** "Initial signal" – The student has some understanding but may lack fluency or multi-step application. Worth reviewing.
* **2/2 in a category:** "Likely secure" – The student demonstrates basic competence in this quick screening. Monitor during regular instruction.

## Language and Framing Guidelines
* **DO NOT** use words like "diagnosed", "failed", "deficit", or "clinical".
* **DO** use phrases like "likely gap", "quick screening", "initial signal", and "restart point".
* **Disclaimer to always include:** "This is a 10-question quick screening designed to identify practical restart points for learning recovery. It is not a comprehensive diagnostic assessment. Teachers should use their professional judgment to adjust plans based on classroom observation."
