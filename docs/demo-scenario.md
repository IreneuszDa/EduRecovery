# Demo Scenario

## Demo Persona

Teacher:

- name: Olena
- role: volunteer math educator
- context: supporting displaced Ukrainian students in a mixed-level classroom
- technical confidence: low to medium

Student:

- age: 12
- grade: 6
- language: Ukrainian
- context: interrupted schooling and recent move between education systems

## Demo Setup

Teacher selects:

- subject: mathematics,
- grade: 6,
- age: 12,
- language: Ukrainian plus English or Polish,
- daily study time: 20 minutes,
- classroom mode: disrupted learning,
- available resources: print-first / low-tech.

## Intended Test Result

The demo should intentionally show the student struggling with:

- percentages,
- word problems.

The student should do better on:

- fractions,
- geometry,
- equations.

## Demo Story

1. Olena opens EduRecovery.
2. She chooses the student's grade, language, and learning context.
3. The student completes a 10-question quick screening.
4. EduRecovery shows that percentages and word problems are likely gaps.
5. The app recommends a restart point.
6. The app generates a 14-day recovery plan.
7. Olena opens the Teacher Recovery Brief.
8. She prints the plan and first exercises.

## What Jurors Should Understand

- The teacher did not need to write a prompt.
- The tool gave a specific restart point.
- The output is practical for tomorrow's lesson.
- The product fits disrupted and low-tech education contexts.
- The MVP is feasible and scalable.

## Demo Closing Line

Rebuilding Ukraine starts with rebuilding children's access to learning. EduRecovery helps every teacher know where each student should restart.
